# Short-Form Scriptwriting for Vertical Video

Principles for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. Every second fights the scroll — if it doesn't earn attention, it loses it.

## Retention Mechanics

Viewers decide to stay or scroll in under 3 seconds. Three levers hold them:

- **Curiosity Gap**: Create a question the viewer needs answered. Open a loop in the first sentence; close it later. Never answer the hook in the hook itself.
- **Pattern Interrupt**: Break the expected rhythm. A pause, a cut to something unexpected, a visual that doesn't match what they heard. The brain snaps back to attention when patterns break.
- **Open Loop**: Promise value without delivering it. "The third tool changed everything" — now they need to see the third tool. List structures (3 things, 5 mistakes) are open-loop machines.
- **Dopamine Rhythm**: Short, punchy sentences. Vary pace — fast delivery then a pause. Predictable rhythm = predictable scroll-away.

## Hook Patterns (First 0-3 Seconds)

Hook on-screen text MUST be ≤ 8 words. The hook VO and on-screen text should work together but not repeat each other.

### Question Hook
Pose a question that creates a knowledge gap. The viewer's brain can't leave an open question unanswered.
- "Why do 90% of startups fail in year one?"
- "What if your morning routine is killing your focus?"
- "You know that thing where you..." (relatable question)

### Controversial Take
State something against common belief. Triggers emotional response — agreement or disagreement, both keep them watching.
- "Your 5-year plan is sabotaging your career"
- "Hard work is a trap"
- "Stop networking. Start building."

### Surprising Fact / Stat
Lead with a number or fact that shocks. The brain flags unexpected information as worth processing.
- "The average founder works 14 hours and ships nothing"
- "3 tools replaced my entire team"
- "This one metric predicts startup failure"

### Relatable Moment
Call out a universal experience. "That's me" triggers instant identification.
- "POV: You just realized you spent 6 months building the wrong thing"
- "Me explaining my job to my parents"
- "When someone asks what I do all day"

### Pattern Interrupt
Break the visual/audio pattern to force attention. Often visual-first — the VO doesn't match what you see, or there's a sudden absence of sound.
- Text reveal that contradicts VO
- Sudden silence then a statement
- Camera angle change + controversial line

## Retention Beat Structure (30s Video)

| Beat | Time | Type | Purpose |
|------|------|------|---------|
| Hook | 0-3s | hook | Stop the scroll. Curiosity gap or pattern interrupt. Max 8 words on screen. |
| Body 1 | 3-10s | point | Deliver on the hook's promise. First insight, tool, or takeaway. |
| Body 2 | 10-18s | point / stat | Second insight. Change pace — stat, example, or deeper layer. |
| Rehook | 18-22s | rehook | Pattern interrupt to prevent mid-video drop. "But here's what nobody talks about..." |
| CTA | 22-28s | cta | Clear action. One ask only. Never "like, comment, subscribe, share" — pick one. |
| End Card | 28-30s | cta | Branded close with follow CTA text on screen. |

For 30s: target 4-6 beats. Fewer beats = deeper retention per beat. More beats = faster pace.
For 15s: target 3-4 beats (hook, body, CTA).
For 45-60s: 6-8 beats; add a third body point and a second rehook.

## CTA Patterns

One ask per video. Stacking CTAs dilutes action.

- **Follow loop**: "Follow for more [topic] tips" — hooks them into your content series
- **Curiosity next**: "I'll cover [related topic] tomorrow" — serial open loop
- **Comment bait**: "Which tool would you try first?" — low-friction engagement
- **Save / share**: "Send this to a founder who needs to hear it" — social proof driver
- **Link in bio**: "Full breakdown in bio" — only when you have a real link

CTA VO should be 1-2 sentences. CTA on-screen text: 2-4 words max ("Follow →", "Save for later", "Link in bio").

## Word Economy

**On-Screen Text**: 8 words max per beat. Text competes with VO — they must complement, not duplicate. If VO says it, on-screen text should show it differently (numbers, keywords, contrast).

**VO Pacing**: ~3 words per second conversational. 30s video = ~90 VO words total. Write like you talk, not like you write. Contractions, sentence fragments, pauses.

**Rules**:
- No sentence over 12 words in VO
- On-screen text: concepts, not sentences. "Ship faster" not "This tool helps you ship faster"
- Numbers: digits, not words. "3 tools" not "three tools"
- Remove filler: "actually", "basically", "you know", "I think"
- One idea per beat. If a beat has "and" connecting two ideas, split it

## Brand Voice Integration

Match the brand's tone list without sacrificing conversational punch:
- **Bold**: State opinions as facts. No hedging.
- **Direct**: Subject-verb-object. No passive voice.
- **Warm**: Use "you" and "we". Personal, not corporate.
- **Witty**: One sharp observation per script, not a joke per line.
- **Professional**: Facts over feelings. Credentials in subtext, not stated.

Never sound like a brand. Sound like a smart friend who happens to know a lot about the topic.

## Visual Intent

Every beat needs a `visual_intent` that describes what the viewer SEES — not layout, not design decisions:
- Good: "Close-up of phone screen with notification count climbing"
- Good: "Split screen: messy desk vs clean workspace"
- Bad: "Use brand colors with centered text"

Visual intent should reinforce the beat's message or create productive tension (VO says one thing, image shows another).
